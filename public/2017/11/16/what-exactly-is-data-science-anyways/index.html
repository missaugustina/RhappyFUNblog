What exactly is data science anyways?

2017/11/16

Once upon a time there was a little girl with a big personality and an even bigger imagination. Attracted to the weird and unusual, she preferred her own intuitions to inflexible instructions and always asked adults hard questions (but, why!?). Fast-forward to adulthood and it’s no wonder I ended up in data science.

Like “DevOps” several years before it, “Data Science” has become yet another trendy, ambiguous tech industry term. What I find intriguing is that “Data Science” is a tech industry term that expands beyond the traditional boundaries of the technology industry.

Depending on who you ask, a “Data Scientist” means something different.

So, what do Data Scientists do, exactly?

According to the O’Reilly Data Science Salary Survey (2016), data scientists do a lot of things!

Kaggle also did a survey and provided the results as an open dataset.

https://www.kaggle.com/kaggle/kaggle-survey-2017/downloads/freeformResponses.csv https://www.kaggle.com/kaggle/kaggle-survey-2017/downloads/conversionRates.csv

According to the distribution of respondants, many appear to come from a computer science background which may contribute to this perception of data science.

What can job postings tell us? When companies are looking to hire data scientists, what terms are they using? Stack Overflow’s job postings appear to also have a tech-centric view of data scientists.

It is naive to assume that these tech-centric sources are the only perspectives worth considering when seeking to define the elusive Data Scientist. The R community hosts several conferences with useR being one of the major ones that bring R users together from across the spectrum.

What topics were presented at the last useR conference? Maybe this can give us a better idea of what it means to Science the Data!

useR topics

Meetup.com is a website that provides a platform for coordinating user groups. Using a similar analysis strategy, maybe looking at the topics presented at R meetups can give us more insight into this chameleon of a character.

Contemplations like this often begin with a literal dictionary definition of the terms they are contemplating. I find ending with this to be more appropriate here because ultimately it shows how meaningless the title “Data Scientist” really is.

“Scientist” and “Science”

These criteria include an organized body of knowledge, a track record of non-obvious discoveries, an experimental method to test hypotheses, and an openness to any hypothesis being falsified. They also include secondary distinctions such as interplay between science and art, and between basic and applied.
